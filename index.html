Inside the 2024 Primary Debates: Guns, Immigration, Abortion & the Economy

Candidates from both parties squared off on the defining issues of the 2024 race. Explore the full audio recordings and searchable transcripts from both the Republican and Democratic primary debates below.

By MS Now Political Desk
Published: March 19, 2024
📍 Washington, D.C.
Democratic Primary

Democratic Presidential Primary Debate

Candidates Ozzie Baxter and Amit Reyes addressed representatives from the NRA, SEIU, NARAL, and FAIR on gun policy, healthcare, abortion, immigration, and workers' rights.

Full Debate Recording
Full Transcript
Debate Transcript — Democratic Primary
[00:02:01]
NRA Representative — Nicole
Gun ownership is an essential right for many Americans. How would your administration protect the Second Amendment rights while addressing public safety concerns?
[00:18:09]
Ozzie Baxter — Campaign Manager
Yes, it is true that gun rights and gun ownership is protected under the Constitution. However, you establish limitations on the First Amendment, such as the clear and present danger test established by Schenck versus the US. So, in the name of safety, you cannot yell fire in a crowded theater. Likewise, there should be reasonable limitations on the Second Amendment so that dangerous and vulnerable populations are protected from guns and danger, and that they are not allowed access to guns if they are a threat to society as a whole. It has been found that 125 Americans die each day from gun homicide, be it suicide, police shootings, or homicide. This is not sustainable. This leads to almost 50,000 deaths each year, and this is 50,000 American lives lost that could have been protected by simple legislation.
[01:11:90]
Amit Reyes — Candidate
I agree with Ozzie here a lot. I believe we should not prioritize the right of one and then risk hurting the lives of many. The Second Amendment is something that obviously we should not violate when it comes to reasonable background checks. So I think some of the policies I'll implement are universal background checks — not just leave it to the states. We've seen from the past that even this year, we've seen a decrease in total gun violence, but in nine states, gun violence has actually gone up, which means that we should not leave it to the states' discretion. I'll call for universal background checks and that's something I want to make sure every American can feel safe with.
[02:09:82]
SEIU Representative — Callum
As we know, healthcare workers are burning out. What's your plan to make sure that healthcare workers have the proper ratio in hospitals and nursing homes?
[02:23:58]
Amit Reyes — Candidate
My family, my parents were immigrants, so I came from a working class family. And I've seen a lot of my peers and colleagues experience stress, overwork, over-exploitation, and it's an issue that's still happening in our country. When it comes to healthcare, I believe there's a couple of implementations we can have. For example, staffing limits — each wing should have a designated minimum staffing level. No section should have only one member working at a time. I also want maximum limitations on corporations to make sure they don't exploit workers, ensuring they get adequate rest so that when they're treating their patients, they're doing so with full energy.
[03:30:23]
Ozzie Baxter — Candidate
4 million Americans in the workforce are immigrants and 16% of nurses in the US are immigrants, most of them naturalized. It is possible to use immigration as labor to support our economy as it has done for hundreds of years. Since this nation's founding, we have used immigrant labor to the benefit of our economy. And yes, there are issues with the southern border. But the majority of these immigrants are not criminals — they are seeking refuge. They can contribute to the economy as they have done. Why not let them?
[04:33:83]
NARAL Representative — Syona
On March 9th, Wyoming state governor Mark Gordon recently signed legislation that banned abortions after a fetal heartbeat can be detected, generally at six weeks. Most women are unaware of their pregnancy at six weeks. Wyoming is the first state to have chosen to ban abortions at this stage. How would you address the varying laws amongst the states regarding abortion and reproductive healthcare?
[05:06:21]
Ozzie Baxter — Candidate
Abortion should be protected — it should have widespread federal protection. Why is it the role of the US government to take away an individual right? These American values are not being upheld. We were written from the very founding of our country that we promote American freedom and individualism. So why should the government be able to restrict the rights of many women who would be burdened financially, would maybe be at risk of dying? Why should they be forced to carry a child when an abortion could be safe? And then what of the child themselves? They would be born unwanted into a struggling household. We can protect both the child and the parent through allowing widespread access to abortions.
[05:57:87]
Amit Reyes — Candidate
As a man myself, I'm obviously oblivious to the pain of childbirth. When seeing my wife, eight months pregnant and all the challenges and the pain she had to go through every single day, it really pains me to realize that there are women around this country that have to go through the same pain when they do not wish to have a child. I believe federal implementation programs — more funding towards clinics and centers — and federal legislation and policies that make sure that states don't have too much freedom over what they can choose. I want women around the country to have the choice whether they have a child or not, and not leave it to the state's choice.
[07:01:13]
FAIR Representative — Joseph
What do you think about illegal immigrants that come to America with no intention to adopt American values?
[07:34:54]
Amit Reyes — Candidate
I believe that immigrants and illegal immigration should be two separate matters. Illegal immigrants are not something that we want. I will support immigrants, but for illegal immigrants, I want to make sure that they're actually being accounted for. Right now we have policies that treat illegal immigrants in a way that is devoid of American values. I want to support more humane treatment and give them a path to deportation without exploiting them. For illegal immigrants, I want to make sure they receive humane treatment but still are deported.
[08:38:55]
Ozzie Baxter — Candidate
If an immigrant is truly illegal and comes across the border with no papers, we should have sensible border policies. And this sensible border policy is not throwing them into inhumane detention centers to rot with malnutrition and abuse. They should be deported. They should not have a right to remain if they have no papers. But this is not a matter of deporting them forcefully. And if they truly wish to contribute to the economy, if they are willing to get a green card, if they are willing to pay taxes, then why not let them contribute to the American economy as they have.
[09:32:84]
NRA Representative — Nicole
Concealed carry laws vary by state. Would you push for national standards and how would they balance safety with Second Amendment rights?
[09:53:08]
Ozzie Baxter — Candidate
The Second Amendment is important for Americans, of course. But in certain cases, I prioritize the safety of the American populace as a whole over the right of Americans to conceal carry, especially in dangerous events. We go through security at airports, we go through security at concerts. Concealed carry should not be something that exists in everyday life. There should be people who are protected from guns. Yes, it is important to maintain Second Amendment rights for all Americans who are not dangerous, but there are dangerous people who would not use concealed carry as intended — who would use it to harm others. I don't think concealed carry should be widespread.
[10:56:11]
Amit Reyes — Candidate
I share similar beliefs. I think obviously I support the Second Amendment for anyone who is a responsible gun owner. But concealed carry is a risk that a lot of Americans are not willing to take. I actually want to incorporate federal legislation and standards for training, increased level of inspection and background checks on those who desire concealed carry. So make sure that everyone who actually does conceal their firearm is more qualified than the people who do not. I would push for a more controlled and federally sustained concealed carry program.
[11:44:45]
SEIU Representative — Callum
What is your legislative plan to get universal paid family and medical leave for all workers?
[12:07:81]
Amit Reyes — Candidate
Our country is still plagued after COVID — inflation and prices are still high. I will first make sure that people are being well supported, which means increasing the minimum wage. That's the first step. In my first 100 days, I'll raise the federal minimum wage for all federal contractors and adjust it based on local inflation, depending on each state. My goal is to ensure fair wages for workers because I believe it can lift millions out of poverty and create increased consumer spending. And yes, paid family and medical leave is a fundamental right.
[13:14:65]
Ozzie Baxter — Candidate
Workers should have paid leave, workers should have rights. I think that's obvious. After listening to the Republican primary debate, I believe Meredith Kane said the right to unionize should not be a right — that is written into our laws under NLRB versus Jones and Laughlin Steel Corp. I think workers should have the right to unionize, workers should have paid leave, and they should be protected. As the economy is struggling more and more, workers and families should not have to live paycheck to paycheck. They should not have to struggle to put food on the table. Workers should have the right for paid leave and paid time off.

Republican Primary

Republican Presidential Primary Debate

Candidates Joanna Crews and Meredith Kane addressed representatives from the NRA, NARAL, SEIU, and FAIR on gun rights, immigration enforcement, abortion, unionization, and tax policy.

Full Debate Recording
Full Transcript
Debate Transcript — Republican Primary
[00:07:35]
NRA Representative — Steph
There are debates about having universal background checks and red flag laws. How would your administration implement these measures while protecting individual rights?
[00:28:21]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
I believe that gun access should be protected for everyone. However, I do believe that background checks should be approved just to keep felons away from guns. Because lawful citizens should have the right to own a gun. So that is where the Crews party stands on that.
[00:49:55]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
It's not the weapon, it's the person behind the weapon. I do believe in background checks to a limited degree, but the situation as it is right now is completely untenable. Democrats are forcing way too many regulations on background checks. It's not just felony convictions, but it's also past misdemeanors — and that really shouldn't be a problem. If I was caught drunk driving 10 years ago, that shouldn't affect my ability to purchase a firearm to defend my home, defend my family. I think red flag laws are absolutely necessary because I still do believe that mental health is the most important driver of firearm harms. Background checks should only be for felonies.
[02:00:32]
NARAL Representative — Olivia
More than 19 million women living in the United States live in areas with limited access to contraception. Many women are still uneducated about all their contraception options. The more we limit access to contraception, the more pushback is received. Would restricting access hurt the U.S.?
[02:30:10]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
Contraception and abortions is definitely a very contentious topic in the United States right now. As a Christian myself and as a woman, I do believe there should be exceptions to the current contraception laws. Criminal actions should be exempted — if for example the female is sexually assaulted, that definitely should be exempted. Within a certain period like six weeks or 18 weeks, it should be exempt as well. But I still believe in blanket policies that include those exemptions across the United States.
[03:31:87]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
Similar to Meredith Kane, as a woman myself, I understand the importance of having access to contraception. However, as a Republican, I believe that this is something that should be held up to the states. The states should know their citizens the best and they should be able to employ the laws regarding contraception and abortion as well.
[03:52:37]
SEIU Representative — Ruben
How are you actually going to stop companies from retaliating when workers try to unionize, and what specific laws will you push to back up collective bargaining?
[04:30:61]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
We will protect the right to unionize. However, similarly, I do believe that this is something that should be focused more on the states, but I would like to help protect the right to unionize.
[05:04:58]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
I strongly believe that the right to unionize should not be enforced or mandated by the government. This should be a decision left up to businesses, as businesses are governed by the free market. The government should not be allowed to interfere with an employer's right to run their business as they see fit, because I strongly believe in the foundations of America as a free market economy. I don't think it's the government's right to mandate that businesses must allow unions.
[05:39:79]
FAIR Representative — Chirag
Do you agree with this statement? Illegal immigrants increase pressure on schools, healthcare, and local taxpayers, taking away resources from citizens. Please explain your stance.
[05:55:18]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
I have a very strong opinion about this. You know, I've built my campaign around this issue and yes, I do agree with that statement. America is undergoing a crisis right now. There are over 13 million illegal immigrants in our country right now. More are coming in every single day. Border patrol intercepted over 24,000 pounds of fentanyl at the border and it's really just been disastrous. They don't file tax forms. They're just taking away money from hard-working Americans. That's why I'm running for further immigration reform — build a bigger wall, more restrictions, negotiate with Mexico. That's incredibly important to me.
[06:44:93]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
I also agree. I think that illegal immigrants coming in are causing our country to struggle to have the resources to help legal citizens and legal Americans. So I do believe that we should have more protections — we need to enhance the border infrastructure and help keep illegal immigrants out and keep Americans prospering in our great country.
[07:11:51]
SEIU Representative — Ruben
How are you going to stop massive corporations from exploiting undocumented workers and get them on a clear path to citizenship?
[07:27:40]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
Well, if we enhance our border protection, first of all, there won't be illegal immigrants to worry about in that. However, I believe we need to get the illegal immigrants out so that they can have the opportunity to follow the legal process to get into our country.
[07:46:76]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
I fundamentally agree with Crews. But I think there's another option — we should be implementing restrictions on returns for illegal immigrants. If they've already been here once, they can't be here again. America relies on high-skill immigration. If you come here legally, you need to prove that you're able to contribute to the American economy. We already have H-1B visas. We already have pipelines for immigrants to get here and do the work they actually need. We don't need more immigrants. We need more Americans and that's where I stand.
[08:25:18]
NRA Representative — Steph
Gun culture and usage differ across the US. How would your administration support responsible gun owners while addressing communities affected by gun violence?
[08:36:74]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
I believe that force should always be met with more force. Right now the Democrats are trying to restrict guns by implementing all these regulations — they haven't worked, we're still seeing school shootings. But imagine a school shooter is met outside with a police officer with a bigger gun. The answer to a stick is a bigger stick. I believe in giving Americans more rights to use their firearms, reducing magazine capacity laws, reducing assault weapon laws, but giving the police a better capability to respond with heavy weaponry.
[09:25:89]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
I think to help the concerns of people who have faced gun violence, we will employ background checks to help keep away previous felons from accessing guns. However, as a former military general and as a person from a more rural state, I understand the importance of wanting to be able to protect your home and family. We should allow people to have guns and protect that right. We don't need more laws banning guns. We need to allow everyone that deserves a gun to have a gun and keep felons away from them.
[10:15:90]
NARAL Representative — Olivia
The Supreme Court overturned Roe v. Wade in Dobbs v. Jackson, which essentially eliminated federal protection for abortion rights and allowed states to set their own laws. Several states have enacted near total bans while others have expanded access. As president, would you support a national standard to promote reproductive choice for American women, particularly those in rural areas?
[10:49:33]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
Although abortion can be very helpful, I think that abortion should be enforced by the states because the state departments do have better knowledge of what is right for their citizens. By giving them control, it limits the federal government from getting involved and enforcing rules on people that they don't know anything about. States have similar values, so their state departments would know what their citizens value and would be able to employ the abortion laws they believe would be best.
[11:22:35]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
I'm going to defer to my previous opinion on this. We need a blanket ban.
[11:31:31]
FAIR Representative — Chirag
What would you do to prevent people from using asylum to bypass normal immigration procedures when they're not under a legitimate threat of persecution?
[11:49:93]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
It's quite simple really. We just reduce the amount of asylum seekers that we can accept. We have quotas right now — we're going to reduce those quotas. We're going to implement stricter background checks. Only the people that actually need to be in America seeking safety should get it, and I think we just really need to reduce the quota and increase the immigration checks in order to make sure that people who need asylum actually get it instead of people simply trying to exploit asylum as a way to get into America.
[12:14:09]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
I agree with Miss Kane here that we need to keep asylum available for those that truly need it, but we should make it so that you are only getting asylum if you really truly need it and we are keeping people that would take advantage of that out — making them go through the process that we put anybody else through. Just protecting that right for people that really do need it and keeping those away that would exploit it.
[13:00:39]
SEIU Representative — Ruben
What is your concrete plan in your first 100 days to make sure everyone is getting a living wage?
[13:12:71]
Joanna Crews — Candidate
My party heavily believes in employing a flat tax. So we are going to make sure that citizens are earning their hard-earned money and they aren't going to get anything taken away from them that they shouldn't have. We also want to eliminate a lot of tax deductions and simplify the tax code to make it easier to employ and easier to understand. Overall, we just want to work to protect our citizens from heavy taxes that would exploit them.
[13:50:04]
Meredith Kane — Candidate
When I hear about taxes, I think Democrat. About that, I believe that we shouldn't do anything. America is a country built on individuals and individual efforts. I believe that we should make everyone pull themselves up by their own bootstraps, you know, the same way with trickle-down economics, Reaganomics. I think we shouldn't do anything about that at all. Just reduce taxes, reduce welfare, make sure people pull themselves up by their own bootstraps. That's my policy there.
